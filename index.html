Codex Œconomicus

A Chronicle of Economic Thought

❦

The personality of inquiry is the necessary assumption that one can find or create something that captures the truth, and with scientific inquiry, methodology about how one follows to accomplish this is even more important.

The science of economics is no different in this regard. The introduction of something resembling a more modern economic science happened with Adam Smith (1776), but fragments existed before, interested in similar but distinct questions.

The history of economic thought can be summarized as attempting to document and observe these discrete and sometimes continuous evolutions in the science, either from a contemporary or historical perspective or both.

Explicit

Colophon

This codex is a single, self-contained HTML file: no build step, no dependencies beyond three typefaces, and ready to be dropped onto GitHub Pages as it stands. The masthead is set in UnifrakturMaguntia, a blackletter used sparingly — display and versals only, never running text. Headings and rubrics are set in Cinzel, a lapidary roman after inscriptional capitals; the body is EB Garamond.

The red headings are rubrication — the medieval scribe's practice of marking divisions in red lead, from which we get the word rubric. The opening initial is a versal. The arches are drawn in SVG, not borrowed from anywhere.

All content lives in three plain arrays at the foot of the file — TIMELINE, SCHOOLS, and THINKERS — so extending the codex is a matter of adding an entry, not touching the markup.

Candlelight and parchment themes toggle from the masthead. Motion is disabled for readers who prefer reduced motion. The name of the codex, like everything else here, is a placeholder awaiting its author.
