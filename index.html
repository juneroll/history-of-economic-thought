The personality of inquiry is the necessary assumption that one can find or create something that captures the truth, and with scientific inquiry, methodology about how one follows to accomplish this is even more important.

The science of economics is no different in this regard. The introduction of something resembling a more modern economic science happened with Adam Smith (1776), but fragments existed before, interested in similar but distinct questions.

The history of economic thought can be summarized as attempting to document and observe these discrete and sometimes continuous evolutions in the science, either from a contemporary or historical perspective or both.

This codex is a single, self-contained HTML file: no build step, no dependencies beyond three typefaces, and ready for GitHub Pages as it stands. The masthead is set in UnifrakturMaguntia, a blackletter used sparingly — display and versals only, never running text. Headings and rubrics are set in Cinzel, a lapidary roman; the body is EB Garamond. The red headings are rubrication, the opening initials are versals, and the arches are drawn in SVG, not borrowed from anywhere.

Every view has an address. Routes follow the pattern #/chronicle, #/thinkers/adam-smith, #/disputationes/methodenstreit, #/library/wn-i-iii, and filters travel too: #/thinkers?school=chicago. The browser's back button turns the pages.

To bind a folio — an essay behind any thinker or disputation — add a template to the authoring area at the foot of this file: <template id="folio-thinker-adam-smith">…</template> or <template id="folio-disputatio-methodenstreit">…</template>. Plain HTML inside: <p>, <h3>, <blockquote>, <div class="aside"> for a Nota box, <p class="sources"> for the tailpiece. The first paragraph takes the versal automatically, and every unbound card tells you the exact template id it is waiting for.

To add a glossed leaf, append an entry to the LEAVES array: paragraphs are plain strings, and any phrase wrapped as <g n="1">…</g> becomes a highlight keyed to gloss 1 in the margin apparatus. Everything else — TIMELINE, SCHOOLS, THINKERS, DISPUTATIONES — remains a plain array; extending the codex is a matter of adding an entry, never touching markup.

Candlelight and parchment themes toggle from the masthead. Motion is disabled for readers who prefer reduced motion. The Demsetz folio is a bound sample awaiting replacement; the name of the codex, like everything else here, is a placeholder awaiting its author.
